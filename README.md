# Aviation Safety Analysis

## Project Summary
This project analyzes NTSB aviation accident data (1983-2023) to identify the safest aircraft makes and models for an airline insurer client.

## Key Findings

### Small Aircraft Recommendations
- Cessna 172, Cessna 185, and Piper PA-28 show the lowest fatal/serious injury rates
- These models have large maintenance ecosystems and simple designs that contribute to better accident survivability

### Large Aircraft Recommendations
- Boeing 737 and Airbus A320 family show the lowest injury and destruction rates
- Modern jets with advanced safety systems perform significantly better

### Key Factors Affecting Safety
1. **Weather Conditions** - Accidents in IMC (low visibility) result in significantly higher injury and destruction rates than VMC conditions
2. **Number of Engines** - Multi-engine aircraft have lower injury and destruction rates than single-engine aircraft due to redundancy
